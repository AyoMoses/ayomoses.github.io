Note

A minute to learn... A lifetime to master

Yes. A lifetime it is and I'm still not close to a decades' half child. I remember the joy I felt when i deployed my first project on the internet. Pure joy and bliss. The countless deletions and starting all over again. Pulling it all down to redo. Our current stage gives us the mindset of a master piece but I do not wish to look at this development in 6 months and still feel its as beautiful. Rather, I'd want to appreciate the work and beauty I created but also laugh at the mistakes I made and how i should have done it better. That, is growth and what I thirst for each moment.

Being a self-taught takes courage, determination, perseverance, strong-will and denying yourself that fun you used to enjoy. Sitting for hours learning and coding while your folks watch that favourtie TV show (GOT!!!). At least, you get my point. Non programmers do not understand the joy in seeing a code work finally or being able to fix a bug and smiling at your screen or shouting with joy. Solving problem has never been great. This field as I've come to know cares about "what you can do" and its filled with amazing people in the community ready to help. Thank you to all who have been there for me directly and indirectly.

This is the first edition of my portfolio so, I've decided to name it 1.0 as subsequent versions will have its version name. Yeah... you get the vibe now. What you see so far explains my growth and my perception. Animations are what I love and just like yesterday when i learnt my first langauge. I started with Ruby but my laptop deficieny could not allow me focus on Rails so, my mentor Adewole Mayowa prompted me to start HTMl and CSS. Since then, I haven't looked back.

I often feel like giving up since I'm learning the trade. My character is more mature and I've never been this hungry to learn and grow and my goal is to someday, work on a freelance. With my B.A in History/international Relations be aware that I intend to keep each note as i develop a new "point O" of my portfolio. This will be my trainning and integration field. I mean, It is mine and I love what I've been able to achieve in a short time.

Thank you for visiting my page and taking your time to read this. I hope to have more notes with time. In building this one page navigation, I've used some plugins which you might find useful as a developer if you happen to read this or wondering how I did what I did.
