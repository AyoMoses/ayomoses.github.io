Note

A minute to learn... A lifetime to master

Yes! A lifetime it is and I'm still not close to half a decade. I remember the pure joy and bliss I felt when i deployed my first project on the internet. The countless deletions and trials, doing the same thing over and over again. The stage I’m at presently, gives me the mindset of a master piece. However, in about 6months, I would want to look at this stage and appreciate the work and beauty I created but also laugh at the mistakes I made and how i should have done it better. That, is growth and what I thirst for each moment.

Being a self-taught takes courage, determination, perseverance, strong-will and denying yourself that fun you used to enjoy. Sitting for hours learning and coding while your folks watch that favourtie TV show! Non-programmers do not understand the joy in seeing a code work finally or being able to fix a bug and smiling at your screen or shouting with joy. Solving problem has never felt so great.

This field as I've come to know cares about "what you can do" and is filled with amazing people in the community ready to help. To everyone who has been there for me directly and indirectly in one way or the other, I say thank you!

This is the first edition of my portfolio so, I've decided to name it 1.0 as subsequent versions will have their own version name. What you see so far explains my growth and my perception. Animations are what I love and just like yesterday when i learnt my first language Ruby, my laptop deficiency could not allow me focus on Rails so, my mentor Adewole Mayowa prompted me to start HTMl and CSS. Since then, I haven't looked back. Oftentimes I feel like giving up but at the same time, I have the drive to never give up and I've never been this hungry to learn and grow and my goal is to someday, work on a freelance.

With my Bachelors’ Degree in History/International Relations, I intend to keep each note as i develop a new "point O" of my portfolio. This will be my training and integration field. I mean, It is mine and I love what I've been able to achieve in a short time.
